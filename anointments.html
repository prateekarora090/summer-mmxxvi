THE ANOINTMENTS

Thirty-two mortals RSVP’d. None will leave that way.

Instrument of the weekend · all three universes

The doctrine

At an hour of the weekend that will not be announced, each guest is anointed: ordained, by name, a god of something they never asked to govern. A scroll is read aloud. A laurel is placed. There is no appeals process.

The titles are not being invented. They are being transcribed — every guest has already earned theirs, over roughly a decade of documented behavior. The Committee’s task is to write down what everyone already knows and has been too polite to say with ceremony.

The craft

  • An anointment is not a compliment. It is a finding of fact, delivered with full honors.
  • Every title cites its evidence. The lore is the record; the record is long. If it didn’t happen at least twice, it isn’t a domain yet.
  • Specificity is divinity. “God of the Grill” is a draft. “Titan of the Char, Smoke-Anointed, Rare by Decree” is a verdict.
  • A good anointment implies a costume. The Committee is encouraged to weaponize this.
  • Nothing leaves the Vault before the ceremony. A leaked title is void, and must be rewritten harder.

The process

DRAFT→ COMMITTEE PASS→ FINAL

All thirty-two drafts are written and waiting. Every edit signs its editor — the Vault keeps receipts, as is tradition.
