An enchanted forest. One weekend a year.

The deeper you go, the weirder it gets.

Orchard Hill Estate · Harriman, NY · Jul 30 – Aug 2

into the trees ↓
I

The Edge of the Woods

Friday · Jul 31

Friday is easy. Arrive whenever. Claim a bed, test the pool, dinner one at the long table. The woods keep their distance. For now.

After darkSARDINES

Lights out. One of us hides; find them, and you hide with them too. The house goes quiet one person at a time, until someone is walking the dark halls alone. The forest loves this game.

II

The Glade

Saturday · Aug 1 · daylight

Morning. The glade opens: pool, sun, zero obligations. Meet the glade.

The sacred creature

ARTEMIS

Every enchanted forest keeps one. Ours is a cow. In 2024 she was waiting on this lawn when we arrived — and she never left. The forest grew around her. Bring her flowers.

Her festival

THE CARNIVAL

It assembles around her by unknown means: a bouncy castle, limbo, sumo suits, the annual water war. Dignity optional. Prizes unclear.

Her games

THE POOL CONQUEST

The pool crowns one champion: Gorilla in the Middle. Jonny has held the title for as long as anyone can remember. The forest respects this. Challengers welcome and doomed.

Golden hourTHE BANQUET

A long table appears at the treeline: thirty-two chairs, a real chef, unreal amounts of food. The forest does not do potlucks.

III

The Deep Woods

Saturday · Aug 1 · after dark

At dark, the woods wake up. The night runs in three acts.

Act I · nightfall

THE WAKING

It begins with the light show. Danny has been talking to the trees all year — at nightfall, they answer.

Act II · the dance

THE REVELS

Then the rave: stutter house & bass, glow on every branch, until the forest says stop. It has never said stop. DJ hunt is live ↗

Act III · deeper

THE LIGHTS

Past the last lantern there are lights. Nobody agrees on what they are. Every year someone loses a few hours and comes back different. They always come back. So far.

IV

The Morning After

Sunday · Aug 2

Sunlight. Floats. Testimony compared over coffee — the stories will not match.

MiddayTHE GROUP PHOTO

All of us in one frame. Proof it happened.

Departures in the afternoon. The forest lets you leave. Everyone comes back eventually.

One more thing

The Anointment

At an hour nobody will announce, every guest is anointed: a spirit of something weirdly specific, read from a tree-scroll, crowned on the spot. No appeals. The Committee holds the scrolls. Thirty-two walk in. Thirty-two spirits walk out.

What to wear

Come as the forest imagines you: wings, antlers, flower crowns, moss, glitter, things that glow. Day — garden creature. Night — fairy-struck.
