An enchanted forest. One weekend a year.

The deeper you go, the weirder it gets.

Orchard Hill Estate · Harriman, NY · Jul 30 – Aug 2

into the trees ↓
I

The Edge of the Woods

Friday · Jul 31

Friday is easy. Arrive whenever. Claim a bed, test the pool, dinner one at the long table. The woods keep their distance. For now.

After darkSARDINES

Lights out. One of us hides; find them, and you hide with them too. The house goes quiet one person at a time, until someone is walking the dark halls alone. The forest loves this game.

II

The Glade

Saturday · Aug 1 · daylight

Morning. The glade opens: pool, water artillery, zero obligations.

The BeastGORILLA IN THE MIDDLE

Every forest has a Beast. The glade is his; the title is Jonny’s. Challengers welcome and doomed.

The GroveARTEMIS

Every enchanted forest has a sacred creature. Ours is a cow. Her name is Artemis. She has always been here. Bring her flowers.

The apparatusTHE BOUNCY MUSHROOM

A castle-sized toadstool, inflated by unknown means. Bounce until the forest spins.

Golden hourTHE BANQUET

One long table, thirty-two chairs, set where the lawn meets the trees. A feast appears. Nobody asks who set it.

III

The Deep Woods

Saturday · Aug 1 · after dark

At dark, the woods wake up.

NightfallDANNY’S LIGHT SHOW

He’s been talking to the trees all year. At nightfall, they answer.

Night → lateTHE REVELS

The rave. Stutter house & bass, glow on every branch. The dance ends when the forest says so. DJ hunt is live ↗

Past the last lanternTHE LIGHTS

Deeper in, there are lights. Nobody agrees on what they are. Every year somebody loses a few hours and comes back different. They always come back. So far.

IV

The Morning After

Sunday · Aug 2

Sunlight. Floats. Testimony compared over coffee — the stories will not match.

MiddayTHE GROUP PHOTO

All of us in one frame. Proof it happened.

Departures in the afternoon. The forest lets you leave. Everyone comes back eventually.

One more thing

The Anointment

At an hour nobody will announce, every guest is anointed: a spirit of something weirdly specific, read from a tree-scroll, crowned on the spot. No appeals. The Committee holds the scrolls. Thirty-two walk in. Thirty-two spirits walk out.

What to wear

Come as the forest imagines you: wings, antlers, flower crowns, moss, glitter, things that glow. Day — garden creature. Night — fairy-struck.
